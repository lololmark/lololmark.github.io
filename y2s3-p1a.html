Book Analysis

A Song to Drown Rivers

YearYear 2
SemesterSemester 3
TypeBook Analysis
CourseDesign Studio VI
Description

A book analysis of A Song to Drown Rivers, exploring how narrative, cultural memory, and water as metaphor can inform architectural thinking and spatial design.

The novel's layered relationship with water — as life-giver, boundary, myth, and destroyer — is read through an architectural lens. Key passages are analysed for their spatial qualities: how the author constructs scenes of enclosure and exposure, intimacy and vastness, stillness and flood.

This literary analysis directly informs the subsequent community library design, where the Sungai Gisir river becomes both site and narrative device — a flowing thread of cultural memory through the urban fabric of Lembah Keramat.
